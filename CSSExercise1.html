Apple (Ringo, りんご / リンゴ)

Japanese people use this noun to refer to an apple or apples. Its kanji expression is very difficult not only for Japanese learners, but for Japanese people as well. So, the hiragana expression is very acceptable. It’s also worth mentioning here that the katakana expression, “リンゴ”, is sometimes used.. Ringo in kanji, The kanji expression of “ringo” consists of the following two characters: 林 : a kanji character used to mean woods in Japanese. 檎 : a kanji character derived from “禽” which means bird or birds in the old Chinese language.
